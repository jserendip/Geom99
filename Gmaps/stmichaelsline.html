The Line of Saint Michael

Thought by some to be a mystical "ley line" that extends from Ireland through to Turkey
the "Line of Saint Michael" is a line along which numerous monasteries and sacred sites
can be found, all of which are dedicated to the Archangel Michael, and many of which
also have legendary origins in which the Archangel appeared to some religious or political
figure and instructed them to build in the given location. It is an interesting coincidence
that these all fall in such a direct line even though made over centuries by different cultures.
While making this story map I discovered that they LOOK very linear on a projected map, but the
actual coordinates fall under an arc, not in a direct line!

Skellig Michael

Though exact dates are not known, the evidence suggests there has been a monastery
dedicated to Saint Michael at Skellig Michael in Ireland since the 8th Century.
You may recognize these structures from the film "Star Wars: The Rise of Skywalker"

Saint Michael's Mount

Monastic buildings were built in the 12th century on this island
off the coast of Cornwall. Notice how similar this area is
to Mont St. Michel in France!

Mont Saint Michel

Like Saint Michael's Mount in England, Mont St. Michel is built on a hill on a small island.
This monastery was supposedly built here at the direct instruction of the Archangel Michael
in the year 708AD Included is a "streetview" from inside the chapel.

Santuario di San Michele del Gargano

Supposedly begun as a monastery around the year 490 when the Archangel Michael
appeared several times to the Bishop of Sipontum in Foggia Italy.
Although not on the seashore, it is also built on a promontory of rock.
Also included is a map showing directions from Santuario si San Michele Gargano to
Normandy where Mont St. Michel is found, highlighting that the development in this
area of Europe also somewhat follows this "line".
